Liver cirrhosis is a late-stage, chronic liver disease where healthy liver tissue is replaced by scar tissue (fibrosis) due to long-term injury.
Caused by conditions like hepatitis or alcohol abuse, this scarring hinders the liver's ability to process nutrients, detoxify blood, and produce proteins.
While irreversible, treatments can slow progression, and early detection is crucial to manage serious, life-threatening complications.

Diagnosis & Management: Detected via blood tests, imaging, or biopsy. Management focuses on treating the underlying cause, lifestyle changes, and, in severe cases, liver transplantation.
